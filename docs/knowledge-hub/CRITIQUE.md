## Gaps

- **Generative-AI tool verticals (image/video/coding/audio)** — No dedicated pages for AI image generation (Midjourney/DALL-E/Stable Diffusion), AI video (Sora/Runway/Veo), AI coding assistants (Copilot-coding/Cursor), or AI voice/audio (ElevenLabs). These are among the highest-volume AI searches in every language and are only glancingly folded into /best-ai-tools/.  
  _Fix:_ Add a Tools sub-cluster: best AI image generators, best AI video generators, best AI coding assistants, best AI writing tools, AI meeting/transcription assistants — each answer-first + comparison table, feeding the directory.
- **Missing brand/vendor coverage** — Perplexity, Grok (xAI), DeepSeek and Mistral have real demand but get no product deep-dive; Perplexity and Grok appear only as mentions. The 'repeatable comparison format' is instantiated only 1-2 times.  
  _Fix:_ Add Perplexity-for-business and (optionally) Grok/DeepSeek pages, and build out 5-6 high-demand two-way comparisons (Copilot vs ChatGPT, Gemini vs ChatGPT, Perplexity vs ChatGPT, Claude vs ChatGPT) using the reusable template.
- **High-demand informational queries absent** — No coverage of 'will AI take my job / AI and employment', 'free AI tools', 'AI hallucinations explained' (glossary-only), 'is ChatGPT safe/free', 'AI statistics 2026'. These are massive top-of-funnel and citation-bait queries.  
  _Fix:_ Add a small set of evergreen explainers + an 'AI statistics 2026' citation-magnet dataset page (strong GEO/link asset) and a 'free AI tools' filter view on the directory.
- **Industry coverage gap: manufacturing/industry** — Swiss/DACH economy is heavily industrial (Industrie 4.0, precision manufacturing, logistics), yet no AI-for-manufacturing / logistics / construction playbook exists in the Industry cluster.  
  _Fix:_ Add /ai-fuer-industrie-fertigung/ (manufacturing) and consider logistics — a defensible, low-competition Swiss/DACH gap.
- **BOFU proof / case studies** — The one case-study page is a framework only; an 'authority hub' selling implementation services with zero real, quantified case studies is weak for conversion and E-E-A-T.  
  _Fix:_ Prioritize securing 2-3 permissioned real case studies; until then run a transparent measurement-framework + anonymized-outcome page and say plainly it is illustrative, not fabricated.
- **Automation-platform tooling** — Agents/automation is conceptually deep but there is no concrete coverage of the platforms buyers actually use (n8n, Make, Zapier AI, workflow orchestration), which is where implementation intent converts.  
  _Fix:_ Add a 'best AI automation/workflow platforms' page bridging the agents cluster to Weissmann build services.
- **Scope realism / sequencing** — ~9 clusters x ~11 pages x 4 languages ≈ 350-400 URLs. Launching in parallel risks thin content, broken freshness commitments and diluted authority for a two-person-led team.  
  _Fix:_ Ship one pilot cluster end-to-end (content + schema + assets + 4-lang), template the pipeline, then scale cluster-by-cluster on a freshness-sustainable cadence.

## Quality risks

- Case studies: highest 'never invent' exposure. An authority + services hub with no real, quantified, permissioned case studies undercuts both E-E-A-T and conversion; the framework-only fallback must be visibly labelled illustrative to avoid implying fabricated proof.
- Volatile vendor data: ChatGPT/Claude/Gemini/Copilot subscription and API prices, plus benchmark scores (MMLU, SWE-bench, Chatbot Arena Elo), churn monthly. Any hardcoded figure will go stale and become an anti-citation signal — enforce 'as of <date>, per <official source>' + outbound links, never assert.
- Cited statistics need verification: AXA 22%->34%, Digitalswitzerland 67% by end-2026, EY 89%, Deloitte CH figures are asserted across the plan. Each must be traced to a dated primary source and stored in one shared 'claims register' so the same number isn't restated inconsistently across 4 languages — otherwise NEEDS VERIFICATION.
- E-E-A-T authorship strain: two named generalist bylines (Giovanna Carpi, Nicola Moessner) authoring deep legal (revDSG/EU AI Act), medical (patient safety/SaMD/MDR) and financial content across every cluster is thin for demonstrable topical expertise; assistants down-rank shallow authority. Consider expert review/attribution for regulated verticals and real credentialed bios.
- Legal-content accuracy: revDSG/FADP and EU AI Act pages risk drifting into 'legal advice' and misstating provisional items (e.g. Digital Omnibus deferral of high-risk deadlines is pending). Requires visible 'legal status as of <date>', disclaimers, and re-review discipline.
- Neutrality vs self-promotion: the KI-Telefonassistent 'Anbieter-Vergleich' and 'best AI tools' pages must stay genuinely neutral — AI engines avoid citing self-serving marketing copy, so a Weissmann-favouring ranking would forfeit the citeability the whole GEO strategy depends on.
- Four-language parity at scale: 4x volume invites machine-translated thin pages. Poorly localized DE/EN/IT/FR duplicates hurt E-E-A-T and can trigger doorway/duplicate signals; each language needs genuine localization (CHF, cantonal, .ch context), not translation.
- Freshness sustainability: the plan promises 'living pages' with last-verified stamps across 350-400 URLs. Broken/stale 'last updated' dates are worse than none — the freshness commitment must be tooled and staffed before scaling volume.
- Demand labelling: many pages are tagged very-high/high demand without measured data. Fine if consistently labelled as estimates/tiers per the hard rules, but any slip into asserting search volumes breaks the 'never fabricate' rule.
