# LinkedIn Authority & Inbound: Principles to Adapt (Not Copy)

## 0. The one strategic idea underneath everything

Inbound comes from **authority + repeat visibility to the same cluster**, not from virality. The 2026 algorithm rewards *predictable* posting to an audience that keeps re-engaging, and it demotes overtly promotional content while boosting educational/behind-the-scenes material. That is unusually good news for your actual situation: an early operator with **strong live demos and an honest "still building" story** is exactly what the current feed rewards. You don't need customer logos to win here — you need specificity, a clear "who I help," and a real product to show.

**Your structural advantage to lean on:** you can *show working AI*, not talk about it — the Weissmann demo line (+41 56 539 18 67), the 24h real-call audio, and the Even Realities G2 live-translation/captions in the showroom. Most "AI thought leaders" can only theorise. That's your moat; the principles below all point at it.

## 1. Headline — the highest-leverage 220 characters

**Principle (from solo B2B operators & coaches):** the winning formula is **"who I help + the specific outcome + the mechanism/category,"** loaded with a concrete noun, not a job title. "Founder | AI" is invisible; "I help Swiss SMEs stop missing calls with an AI phone assistant that speaks Swiss German" is a targeted value prop. Specificity is the only differentiator in an AI-saturated feed, and headlines that *invite a conversation* beat headlines that chase impressions.

**Adaptable patterns:**
- Lead with the audience + pain, then the mechanism: `Helping [Swiss SMEs / clinics / trades] never miss a call — AI phone assistant, DE/EN/FR/IT + Swiss German` .
- The headline is one line but you wear **two hats** — resolve it, don't hide it. Anchor on your primary role (Founder, Weissmann AI) and signal the second cleanly: `… | Official Swiss partner for Even Realities smart glasses`. Never let the phrasing imply you founded AI-Eyewear/Even Realities.
- Put a **proof-of-realness token** where a metric would normally go, since you (honestly) have no customer metrics yet: `Live demo line, not slides` or `Try the AI on a real call`. This substitutes a *verifiable* claim for a fabricated one.

## 2. About section — first-person story, forward-facing, ends in a next step

**Principle (from founder personal-brand playbooks):** the About section is the only first-person real estate on the profile; treat it as **Hook → Value & Proof → CTA**, written forward-looking ("here's what I'm building and who it's for"), not as a past-tense résumé. Narrative-structured profiles report materially more positive interaction. The first ~2 lines matter most because they're what shows before "…see more."

**Adaptable structure:**
1. **Hook (line 1–2):** a sharp problem statement in your customer's own words — e.g. the missed-call / after-hours-lead problem for Weissmann; the "translation without pulling out your phone" problem for the glasses.
2. **Who I help + why me:** state both roles explicitly and *why they connect* (you operate AI in the real world for Swiss businesses and consumers — one as founder, one as the authorised Swiss partner). Naming the second role as "partner/reseller" here also protects you from role-creep later.
3. **Proof you can honestly give:** live demo line, real-call audio within 24h, data stays in CH/EU (nDSG/GDPR), Swiss pricing/no customs on the glasses, physical showroom addresses. These are *facts*, not testimonials — that's the point.
4. **Build-in-public honesty line:** something like "early, transparent, and showing the real product as I grow" sets expectations and pre-empts the "where are your customers?" question by owning the stage you're at.
5. **CTA:** one specific next step (book a 15-min demo / call the line / visit the showroom), not "visit website."

## 3. Featured section — treat it as your funnel's shop window, not a link dump

**Principle (from B2B lead-gen funnel guides):** Featured sits "above the fold"; a completed one correlates with meaningfully more profile views and connection requests. Pin **one lead magnet, one proof/case-style asset, one booking link — and put the highest-value action in position 1**, because most visitors never scroll past it.

**Adaptable patterns for your two-business reality:**
- **Position 1 = your strongest verifiable asset.** For you that's likely a short **demo artefact** (the AI-call audio / a 30-sec product-flow clip), because it converts skeptics faster than any PDF.
- **Position 2 = a "teardown" or explainer** you *can* create truthfully today: "How an AI phone assistant handles a Swiss-German call, step by step" or "What the G2 actually does in a live conversation." This substitutes an educational asset for the case study you can't yet write.
- **Position 3 = one booking/contact CTA** phrased as a specific offer ("Book a 15-min AI-call demo" / "Book a G2 showroom fitting in Zürich"), not "Visit website."
- Keep Weissmann and AI-Eyewear assets **visually distinct** so a visitor instantly understands these are two offers, one operator.

## 4. Content formats — what's actually performing

**Principle (from analysed founder-content datasets & format rankings):**

| Format | Why it wins in 2026 | How you adapt it truthfully |
|---|---|---|
| **Text-only opinion / POV post** | Strong-hook text now out-performs single images ~30%; personal stories + hot takes drive the most *comments*, and comments lift reach ~15× vs. reactions | Founder POV on Swiss AI adoption, data residency, "why Swiss German breaks most voice AI." You have genuine operator opinions — use them. |
| **Document ("carousel") PDF** | Highest dwell-time format; swiping reads as engagement (~top-tier engagement rates). Lists *with embedded perspective* get saved 4–5× more, and **saves are the single strongest algorithm signal** | Teardown carousels: "5 calls a Swiss clinic loses every week," "What live translation glasses feel like in a real meeting." |
| **Native product-demo video (9:16 / 1:1)** | Product-in-authentic-work-environment videos → ~3× more profile visits; native uploads get ~40% more reach than external links; show **one flow = one benefit**, never a feature tour | This is your unfair advantage — record the real AI call, the real live-caption moment. One outcome per clip. |
| **Transparent "build-in-public" post** | The feed now boosts behind-the-scenes / educational and demotes salesy posts; developers and buyers reward honest founder content | Share the *real* stage: demo requests coming in, what you're learning, product decisions — **without inventing revenue or customers**. Your true numbers (early, pre-revenue) are a credible story, not a weakness. |

**Cross-format hook rule:** the first ~150 characters decide everything. The best hooks are **the exact phrase your buyer uses for their pain** ("We miss calls when we're on the tools" / "I don't want to pull out my phone mid-conversation to translate"). Motivational fluff underperforms; specific cost/friction wins.

**Content pillars (pick 3–5, stay consistent):** e.g. (1) Swiss/EU AI reality & data trust, (2) live demos / "show the product," (3) founder build-in-public, (4) practical AI for SMEs, (5) the wearable-AI consumer angle. Pillars are what turn scattered posts into perceived authority.

## 5. Cadence & the engagement routine (where solo operators actually win)

**Principle (from solo-operator & B2B service playbooks):** **3–4 high-quality posts/week beats daily volume** — each post needs room to "breathe" and accumulate engagement, and consistency to the *same cluster* matters more than any single viral hit. Tue/Wed/Thu see the highest engagement.

**The daily 20–30 min routine that compounds:**
- **Protect the "golden hour."** Reply to *every* comment on your own post within ~1–2 hours; early engagement determines how far the post travels.
- **Comment on ~10 posts/day** from your ICP (Swiss SME owners, clinics, trades, tech-curious consumers) — add a real perspective or question, don't "great post 👍."
- **Warm-DM anyone who engaged twice this month** with a *non-pitch* message.
- **Save 5 prospects/day** to a list for follow-up.
- 6–12 months of this reliably out-earns cold outreach — that's the realistic timeline to set expectations against.

---

## 6. Profile → DM → booking funnel mechanics

**Principle (from coaching/consulting funnel guides):** the algorithm penalises engagement-bait and external links hard (external links down-ranked ~60%), so the funnel runs **inside the platform** and stays soft.

**Adaptable mechanics:**
- **"Comment-to-receive" CTA** works well *and* doubles as qualification: offer a genuinely useful asset ("comment 'DEMO' and I'll send the real AI-call recording") — this generates engagement signals *and* a warm list, while keeping the link out of the post body (drop it in DM/first comment).
- **DM structure that converts = real trigger + value-first opener + soft, ignorable CTA.** First message references their actual comment/post, ~2 sentences, **no ask**. Value before the invite. Let them decline without guilt.
- **Route to your real conversion events:** the demo line, the 24h audio, or a showroom fitting — you have *concrete, in-person/live* endpoints most competitors lack. Make the DM's eventual CTA one of those, not a generic "let's hop on a call."
- Replace any "Visit website" profile button with a **specific, low-friction offer** ("Book a 15-min demo").

---

## 7. Guardrails specific to your setup (do not skip)

- **Role integrity everywhere:** Founder of **Weissmann**; **official Swiss sales partner/reseller** for AI-Eyewear (Even Realities). Every headline, About line, and post must keep that clean — it's also *more* credible, not less.
- **Proof = only what's real:** live demos, the demo line, 24h audio, data-in-CH/EU, Swiss pricing/no customs, showroom addresses. **No invented customers, testimonials, follower counts, or results** — the build-in-public angle is precisely what lets you post daily *without* needing them.
- **Substitute realness for metrics:** wherever a top creator would drop a client stat, you drop a *demonstration*. That's a stronger 2026 signal anyway.
