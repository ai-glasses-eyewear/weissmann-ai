# Information Architecture — Adversarial Stress-Test

> Generated by the IA stress-test agent. Verdict first, then failure modes and fixes.

## Verdict

The IA is strong and largely defensible — the silo + localized-slug + intent-separation core is the right call and the registry/CI governance is genuinely scalable. But it is NOT break-proof. Three structural weaknesses can produce real damage: (1) the Academy cluster taxonomy is many-to-one with the service inventory (one 'marketing-seo-geo' pillar sits above TWO money pages SEO+GEO; one 'agenten-automatisierung-voice-chatbots' pillar sits above THREE money pages Agents+Automation+Phone Assistant), so the 'one-keyword-one-URL' contract is asserted but not actually satisfiable as clustered — the highest cannibalization exposure on the site. (2) The four-language-sync invariant as a hard publish gate collides with the thin-content defense: forcing IT/FR parity on 36 Academy pillars + up to 126 matrix cells will either block launch or force machine translation that reads as thin/duplicate to locale-specific quality raters. (3) x-default → German root under-serves the international/AI-assistant audience the whole GEO strategy is built to win, and the generic de/en/it/fr locales ignore Swiss (de-CH/fr-CH/it-CH) targeting for a Zurich-first business. Endorse with the fixes below; do not ship the migration until the cluster-to-service keyword map is closed and case studies are real.

## Cannibalization risks

- Academy cluster 'marketing-seo-geo' is a SINGLE informational pillar sitting above TWO commercial pages (/leistungen/seo/ and /leistungen/generative-engine-optimization/). The one-keyword-one-URL contract cannot hold when one pillar informs two money pages — the 'GEO' head term especially is contested three ways (service page + this cluster + any GEO glossary term).
- Academy cluster 'agenten-automatisierung-voice-chatbots' is ONE pillar above THREE money pages (AI Agents, AI Automation, AI Phone Assistant). 'Voice-chatbots' informational content overlaps directly with the AI Phone Assistant commercial page ('Was ist ein KI-Telefonassistent' vs 'KI-Telefonassistent Zürich').
- AI Agents (generic) vs AI Phone Assistant (declared instance-of): queries like 'KI Agent Telefon' / 'Voice AI Agent' legitimately match both; the isSubTypeOf schema helps machines but not Google's query-to-URL selection.
- Automation vs Agents vs Integrations scopes are crisp on paper but real buyer language blurs them — 'KI-Automatisierung' colloquially includes agentic workflows; four money pages competing for overlapping modifier queries (Anbieter/Kosten/entwickeln lassen).
- Service×Industry matrix page vs Industry hub: /leistungen/ki-telefonassistent/gesundheitswesen/ vs /branchen/gesundheitswesen/ both pull 'KI Gesundheitswesen' intent; the industry hub must stay strictly sector-framing or it cannibalizes its own children.
- Glossary term pages vs Academy cluster pillars: a 'KI-Agent' DefinedTerm page and the agents cluster pillar both target the definitional/informational query — two informational URLs for one head term, the exact failure the contract forbids.
- Comparisons library ('Automatisierung vs Agenten') vs the same distinction rendered in Academy pillars and in service disambiguation blocks — comparison intent is expressed in three places.

## Thin-page risks

- Service×Industry matrix is 9×14=126 cells; sourcing ~800–1000 words of GENUINELY industry-specific pain/workflow/proof per cell is unrealistic — word-count gates pass while pages stay semantically templated/near-duplicate.
- 4× language multiplication of every page: IT and FR versions produced by machine translation to satisfy the sync gate are the classic thin/duplicate signal to locale-specific quality evaluation.
- 36 Academy pillars (9 clusters × 4 languages) launched together under four-language-sync must each be substantive — high risk of thin FR/IT pillars gating or diluting the whole authority engine.
- Glossary is thin BY DESIGN (1–2 sentence answer-first definitions) at scale — a large DefinedTermSet of near-stubs risks looking like a thin-content farm without supporting body content.
- GEO service page for an SME audience may have genuinely little unique commercial substance vs the SEO page — thin/overlap on a low-volume emerging term.
- Sub-industry leaf pages (dental, physiotherapy) nested under the service silo can fall below the uniqueness bar yet still be published as sections.
- Company 'Privacy & Security' trust page split from legal /datenschutz/ risks being thin marketing filler if it has no distinct substantive content.
- 'section-only' backlog combos rendered on the parent service's 'Industries we serve' block can bloat that page into a thin link farm if many stubs render there.

## URL-depth risks

- Matrix pages hit 4 path segments plus locale prefix: /en/services/ai-phone-assistant/healthcare/ — these long-tail landing pages sit deeper than the doc's stated '≤3 clicks to money pages' promise, diluting crawl priority and internal PageRank to exactly the pages meant to capture long-tail.
- Every non-German locale carries an extra prefix segment (/en/…, /it/…, /fr/…), so all IT/FR/EN URLs are structurally one level deeper than their DE equivalents — asymmetric depth and equity distribution across languages.
- If Academy clusters gain child articles, depth reaches /en/resources/ai-academy/{cluster}/{article}/ = 5 segments — deep, weakly linked, orphan-prone.
- Deep matrix + deep academy paths compound crawl-budget dilution at hundreds of pages, precisely where the site claims scalability.

## hreflang / i18n risks

- x-default → German root mis-serves international and AI-assistant traffic — the GEO strategy targets global AI citation, but x-default routing sends untargeted/global queries to German. English x-default is likely stronger for the stated GEO goal.
- Generic de/en/it/fr ignore Swiss targeting: a Zurich-first business serving Switzerland arguably wants de-CH / fr-CH / it-CH region signals; plain language codes leave Swiss-intent ranking on the table and may collide with de-DE competitors.
- The slug-localization migration 301s existing EN/IT/FR German-slug URLs to new localized slugs — any missing reciprocal return tag silently invalidates the whole cluster; round-trip validation across 4 locales × every page is the single highest-risk migration step.
- Four-language-sync-as-publish-gate structurally prevents partial clusters (good) but forces either machine-translated thin pages to meet the gate or a full publishing freeze — the invariant trades hreflang completeness for content quality or velocity.
- Legal slugs localized 'last' with German aliases 301'd means legal pages carry incomplete hreflang clusters during that window.
- GEO slug identical across all four languages (generative-engine-optimization) is fine structurally but demands the locale prefix be the sole disambiguator — trailing-slash and absolute-URL consistency must be exact or the identical-slug cluster breaks.
- Netlify 24h JS-cache caveat complicates verifying 301/canonical/hreflang changes in the field, widening the window where a broken cluster goes undetected.

## Navigation overload risks

- Resources mega-menu is dangerously dense: exposing all 9 Academy clusters PLUS Glossary, Guides, Comparisons, Case Studies, Templates, Calculators, Insights in one panel exceeds comfortable scan load — link to the Academy hub instead of enumerating clusters.
- Industries mega-menu lists 14 sectors; combined with the 9-service menu and the dense Resources menu, total exposed choices are high even when grouped.
- Mega-menus cannot surface matrix/leaf pages at hundreds of pages, so discoverability leans entirely on hubs + breadcrumbs + in-body links — any gap orphans important long-tail pages despite CI checks.
- Utility bar crowding: Pricing text link + persistent Demo CTA + language switcher + logo competes for the same header space, worse on tablet.
- Mobile collapse of 5 sections into accordions containing 9+14+~17 items is heavy; the Resources accordion in particular becomes a long scroll.

## Conversion leaks

- Academy/glossary pages own informational intent with 'no pricing in primary content' and only a single reciprocal cross-link as the commercial bridge — high-traffic informational pages with one weak CTA leak the audience the whole authority engine attracts.
- Answer-first blocks optimized for AI Overview/Perplexity citation can satisfy the user in-SERP or in-chat, earning citation without click-through or any conversion path — GEO success that never reaches a CTA.
- Deep matrix pages (4+ segments) that capture long-tail intent are far from proof and pricing modules unless those are duplicated inline; distance-to-conversion grows with depth.
- 'NEEDS VERIFICATION' case-study placeholders shipping on live money pages destroy trust at the decision moment — pages should not index without real proof.
- Split high-intent actions (Demo CTA vs Pricing utility link vs Contact) risk diluting the 'single conversion goal per page' principle the doc itself sets.
- Language switcher must resolve to the equivalent localized page via the registry — if it falls back to locale homepage on any unmapped page, it dumps converting users.
- Calculators are described as 'conversion assist' but no explicit lead-capture mechanism is specified — a common leak where the tool delivers value with no capture.

## Scalability failure modes

- Load-bearing controls are the uniqueness gate + CI checks; if enforcement is relaxed under launch pressure, thin-page explosion across 126 matrix cells × 4 languages (up to ~500 URLs) follows immediately.
- Machine-translation quality across IT/FR at hundreds of pages is the most likely site-wide quality-signal drag; no professional-translation resourcing decision is stated.
- Four-language-sync invariant is a publishing bottleneck — every page blocked on all four locales throttles velocity or forces low-quality parity fills.
- Multiple interdependent registries (slugs/services/industries/matrix/academy/glossary/caseStudies/resources + keyword map) become a concentrated complexity point; one bad data entry propagates to hreflang, schema, breadcrumbs, and sitemap everywhere.
- Content freshness/maintenance decay: hundreds of Academy/glossary/insight pages need ongoing updates or they rot, dragging E-E-A-T.
- Crawl-budget and internal-PageRank dilution as low-value matrix/glossary pages proliferate faster than they earn links.
- Allowing all AI crawlers (GPTBot/ClaudeBot/PerplexityBot/Google-Extended) increases scraping/server load at scale — an accepted tradeoff, but a load factor.
- Operational risk flagged in memory: OneDrive-induced git corruption during large-scale page generation — confirm repo is out of OneDrive before mass generation.

## Must-confirm business inputs

- Real, verifiable case studies / named customers per service and per priority matrix combo — nothing may be invented; unconfirmed renders NEEDS VERIFICATION and should block indexing.
- Which of the 14 industries are genuinely served with real demand + content to publish first, and the launch-priority matrix combos (doc recommends Phone Assistant × Healthcare and × Restaurants).
- Supported integrations/tools for the AI Integrations and AI Automation pages (Make/n8n/Zapier/CRM/ERP connectors) — none may be invented.
- Real pricing in pricing.ts for services beyond phone assistant/websites — otherwise 'request a quote' with NO Offer schema.
- Legal entity data — registered name, legal form, UID (Zefix/Handelsregister) — for Impressum and Organization schema, replacing pending placeholders.
- Approval of the two structural migrations: silo all services under localized /leistungen|/services|/servizi/ with 301 from root /ki-telefonassistent/; and full per-language slug localization with the EN/IT/FR 301 map (incl. preise→pricing/prezzi/tarifs).
- AI Websites slug migration ai-websites→ki-webentwicklung and the ki- German-prefix standard.
- GEO slug (full generative-engine-optimization vs /geo/), Manufacturing DE slug (produktion vs industrie/fertigung), and Academy token per language (ki-academy/ai-academy/academie-ia).
- Whether all 9 Academy clusters launch together across 4 languages, or a subset first — a translation-capacity decision.
- Translation resourcing: professional vs machine for IT/FR (quality vs thin-content tradeoff).
- Swiss locale targeting decision: plain de/en/it/fr vs de-CH/fr-CH/it-CH, and the x-default target (DE root vs EN).
- robots.txt policy for AI crawlers (recommend allow for GEO citeability — conscious owner decision).
- Authoritative sameAs targets once available: Zefix/Handelsregister, Google Business Profile, Wikidata QID, founders' LinkedIn.

## Top fixes

- Close the cluster-to-service keyword map BEFORE building: split 'marketing-seo-geo' into distinct SEO and GEO informational pillars and split 'agenten-automatisierung-voice-chatbots' so each of Agents/Automation/Phone Assistant maps to exactly one informational pillar; make the keyword-to-URL registry the enforced gate at content-brief time, not an assertion.
- Change x-default to English (or add explicit de-CH/fr-CH/it-CH Swiss region targeting) to align routing with the international AI-citation audience the GEO strategy exists to capture.
- Cap the matrix rollout to the two recommended combos live; keep all other combos section-only, and enforce SEMANTIC uniqueness (embedding/near-duplicate check + required real-proof field) in CI, not just an ~800-word count, to stop templated thin pages.
- Resolve the four-language-sync-vs-thin-content conflict explicitly: either fund professional IT/FR translation, or relax the hard publish gate for non-DE while keeping hreflang correct — do not let the invariant force machine-translated thin pages.
- De-densify the Resources mega-menu: link to the Academy hub rather than enumerating all 9 clusters; keep exposed choices per panel bounded.
- Add a real lead-capture/CTA layer to informational Academy, glossary, and calculator pages (inline offer + gated asset + newsletter), not a single reciprocal link, to plug the GEO-citation conversion leak.
- Enforce URL-depth and click-depth budgets: keep matrix at the service/industry level only, flatten Academy so articles never exceed 4 segments, and verify every money page is ≤3 clicks from a locale root.
- Ship the migration as ONE atomic change (301 map + self-canonical + hreflang + llms.txt + sitemap) with full round-trip hreflang validation in staging, scheduling verification around the Netlify 24h JS-cache window.
- Do not index any money or matrix page carrying a NEEDS VERIFICATION placeholder — noindex until a real case study or unique proof block exists.
- Disambiguate the GEO service page from any GEO glossary term and the GEO Academy pillar with a single explicit head-keyword assignment, since GEO is low-volume and most exposed to three-way informational/commercial cannibalization.
